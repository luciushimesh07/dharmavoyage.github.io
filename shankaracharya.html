Adi Shankaracharya

About Shankaracharya

Adi Shankaracharya was the revolutionary philosopher who revived Advaita Vedanta — the doctrine of non-dualism. He travelled across India establishing monasteries and reaffirming the Vedic understanding that the Self (Atman) is identical to the universal consciousness (Brahman).

Five Famous Quotes & Their Meanings

1. "Brahman is the only truth; the world is an illusion."

This expresses the highest Vedantic insight: the world is ever-changing, only the Self remains constant.

2. "You are not the body; you are the pure witness."

Identity with the body causes fear and suffering; freedom comes from seeing oneself as consciousness.

3. "The mind alone is the cause of bondage and freedom."

A disciplined mind becomes liberation; an undisciplined mind becomes suffering.

4. "The Self shines by itself — untouched, eternal."

Awareness is not created; it is revealed when ignorance falls away.

5. "Knowledge destroys ignorance like light removes darkness."

Realization comes not from ritual but through direct insight into one’s true nature.

Shankaracharya’s Philosophy — Summary Table

Concept Essence
Advaita (Non-duality) The Self and Brahman are one; separation is illusion.
Ignorance (Avidya) The root cause of suffering is misidentification with the body-mind.
Knowledge (Jnana) Liberation comes from recognizing the Self as eternal consciousness.
Detachment Freedom requires letting go of desires and aversions.
Meditation Stillness reveals the ever-present reality of the Self.
