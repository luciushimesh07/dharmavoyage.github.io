Vyasa

About Vyasa

Vyasa, also known as Veda Vyasa or Krishna Dvaipayana, is one of the greatest sages in Indian spiritual history. He is revered as the compiler of the Vedas, the author of the Mahabharata, and the composer of the Brahma Sutras. His contributions form the backbone of Hindu philosophical tradition.

Five Famous Quotes & Their Meanings

1. "When dharma declines, chaos rises."

Vyasa emphasizes that the moral order sustains society; the moment it weakens, suffering begins.

2. "Truth alone triumphs."

This reflects his belief that truth is not merely moral but cosmic — aligned with the nature of existence.

3. "The mind is the source of bondage and liberation."

A reminder that the world outside does not bind us — our thoughts do.

4. "Knowledge is the eye of the world."

For Vyasa, wisdom is what allows humans to navigate life with clarity.

5. "Act without attachment, for attachment brings suffering."

This foreshadows the core teaching of the Bhagavad Gita: detached action.
