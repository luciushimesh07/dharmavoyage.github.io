Al-Ghazali

About Al-Ghazali

Al-Ghazali (1058–1111) was one of the greatest Islamic thinkers, known for harmonizing reason, theology, and mysticism. His journey from skepticism to spiritual certainty reshaped the course of Islamic philosophy. He emphasized purification of the heart and practical spirituality.

Five Famous Quotes

1) “Desires make slaves out of kings; patience makes kings out of slaves.”
2) “Knowledge without action is wastefulness; action without knowledge is foolishness.”
3) “The happiness of the drop is to die in the river.” Meaning: True joy is dissolving into the Divine whole.
4) “To get what you love, you must first be patient with what you hate.”
5) “The corruption of the soul is far more dangerous than the corruption of the body.”
