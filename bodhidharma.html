Bodhidharma

About Bodhidharma

Bodhidharma, an Indian monk, brought Zen (Chan) Buddhism to China. He emphasized direct experience over scripture and intense meditation over theory. His teachings form the foundation of Zen’s minimalist, experiential approach.

Five Quotes & Their Meanings

1. “To find a Buddha, you must see your own nature.”

Enlightenment is not outside — it is recognition of what you already are.

2. “The mind is the root; all phenomena are branches.”

Your experience of life depends entirely on your mind.

3. “Not thinking about anything is Zen.”

Zen means direct presence without mental noise.

4. “If you want to be free, get to know your real self.”

Self-knowledge dissolves illusions and suffering.

5. “Those who remain unmoved by suffering are enlightened.”

True wisdom is stable even in difficulty.
