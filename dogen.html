Dōgen

About Dōgen

Dōgen Zenji founded the Sōtō school of Japanese Zen. He taught “Shikantaza” — Just Sitting — a meditation of pure presence. His masterpiece *Shōbōgenzō* explores enlightenment in daily activity.

Five Quotes & Their Meanings

1. “To study the self is to forget the self.”

Self-awareness dissolves ego and reveals true nature.

2. “When you find your place where you are, practice occurs.”

Every moment is a gate to awakening.

3. “The whole moon and the entire sky are reflected in a single drop of dew.”

Each moment contains the totality of existence.

4. “Do not think you will necessarily be aware of your own enlightenment.”

Transformation is subtle, not dramatic.

5. “If you cannot find the truth right where you are, where else do you expect to find it?”

Presence reveals reality more than seeking does.
