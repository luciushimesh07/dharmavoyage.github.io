Epictetus

About Epictetus

Born a slave, Epictetus rose to become one of Stoicism’s greatest teachers. His core message: you can control your actions, thoughts, and attitude — but not external events.

For Epictetus, freedom is internal. True strength comes from mastering desires, accepting reality, and directing effort only where it can make a difference.

Five Famous Quotes

“It’s not what happens to you, but how you react to it that matters.”

Your response determines your freedom.

“No man is free who is not master of himself.”

Self-control is the essence of liberation.

“First say to yourself what you would be; then do what you have to do.”

Clarity followed by discipline creates destiny.

“Wealth consists not in having great possessions, but in having few wants.”

Contentment is the real treasure.

“Circumstances don’t make the man; they reveal him.”

Adversity shows character.
