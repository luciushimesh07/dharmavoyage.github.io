Rumi

About Rumi

Rumi (1207–1273) was a Persian mystic and poet whose teachings revolve around divine love, surrender, inner transformation, and spiritual longing. His poetry dissolves the boundary between human and divine, urging seekers to turn inward and embrace love as the path toward truth.

Five Famous Quotes

1) “What you seek is seeking you.”
Meaning: Your deepest longing is the echo of something already approaching you.
2) “The wound is where the light enters you.”
Meaning: Pain becomes a portal to awareness and spiritual growth.
3) “You are not a drop in the ocean. You are the entire ocean in a drop.”
Meaning: The divine essence exists fully within every individual.
4) “Let yourself be silently drawn by the strange pull of what you really love.”
Meaning: Your heart naturally gravitates toward your true purpose.
5) “Beyond ideas of right and wrong, there is a field. I'll meet you there.”
Meaning: True spiritual union lies beyond judgment and duality.
