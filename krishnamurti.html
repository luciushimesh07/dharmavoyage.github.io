J. Krishnamurti

About J. Krishnamurti

J. Krishnamurti was a radical spiritual philosopher who rejected religious authority, tradition, and all forms of psychological conditioning. He emphasized direct perception, freedom from fear, and observing the mind without any judgment.

Five Quotes & Meanings

1. "To be free is to observe without evaluation."

Judgment clouds perception; clarity comes from pure observation.

2. "It is no measure of health to be well adjusted to a profoundly sick society."

Fitting into a broken system doesn't make one sane.

3. "The observer is the observed."

The division between “me” and “my thoughts” is an illusion.

4. "Truth is a pathless land."

Truth cannot be found through religion, gurus, or methods — only through awareness.

5. "Where there is fear, there is no love."

Love arises only when the mind is free from insecurity.
