Aristotle

About Aristotle

Aristotle, the student of Plato and tutor to Alexander the Great, shaped logic, ethics, biology, metaphysics, rhetoric, and politics. His approach was practical, empirical, and grounded in observation.

Unlike Plato, he believed that reality lies in the physical world — not in abstract ideals. His philosophy emphasizes purpose, reason, and the development of character through balanced living.
