Patanjali

About Patanjali

Patanjali, the father of classical Yoga, authored the Yoga Sutras — one of the most influential texts in spiritual history. He organized the path to inner transformation into the Eight Limbs of Yoga, offering a complete guide to mental discipline, clarity, and liberation.

Five Famous Quotes & Their Meanings

1. "Yoga is the restraint of the mind’s fluctuations."

The goal of yoga is not posture but stillness — freedom from mental turbulence.

2. "Practice becomes firmly grounded when pursued with devotion."

Consistency and sincerity transform practice into realization.

3. "Undisturbed calmness of mind is attained by cultivating friendliness, compassion, joy, and equanimity."

Qualities of the heart shape the clarity of the mind.

4. "When you are inspired, dormant forces awaken."

Inspiration unlocks hidden potential and inner strength.

5. "Pain is inevitable when the mind is attached to the non-eternal."

True peace arises only from anchoring oneself in the timeless Self.

Patanjali’s Philosophy — Summary Table

Concept Essence
Chitta Vritti All mental fluctuations that interrupt inner peace.
Eightfold Path A complete map of ethical, physical, and meditative discipline.
Detachment (Vairagya) Letting go of desire-driven disturbances.
Self-realization Seeing the seer — awareness free from mental noise.
Daily Practice (Abhyasa) Steady effort purifies the mind and stabilizes awareness.
