Socrates

About Socrates

Socrates is considered the founding pillar of Western philosophy. He left behind no written works — instead, his wisdom survives through the accounts of his students, especially Plato. His method was dialogue: questioning assumptions, exposing contradictions, and leading others toward truth through introspection.

Socrates believed that real wisdom is knowing one’s own ignorance, that virtue is the highest good, and that truth should be pursued even at the cost of one’s life — a principle he demonstrated by accepting his death sentence with calm dignity.

Five Famous Quotes

“The unexamined life is not worth living.”

Genuine living requires reflection, self-awareness, and conscious choices.

“I know that I know nothing.”

Wisdom begins when arrogance ends; humility opens the path to truth.

“Be kind, for everyone you meet is fighting a hard battle.”

Compassion is the foundation of a wise society.

“To find yourself, think for yourself.”

True individuality emerges through independent reasoning.

“He who is not a good servant will not be a good master.”

Leadership arises from discipline, humility, and responsibility.
