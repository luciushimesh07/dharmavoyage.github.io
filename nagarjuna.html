Nagarjuna

About Nagarjuna

Nagarjuna, one of Buddhism’s greatest philosophers, founded the Madhyamaka (Middle Way) school. He taught the profound doctrine of *Śūnyatā* — Emptiness — showing that phenomena lack inherent existence. His insights reshaped Mahayana Buddhism and continue to influence global philosophy.

Five Quotes & Their Meanings

1. “Everything is emptiness, and emptiness is everything.”

Reality is free of fixed essence; seeing this brings liberation.

2. “There is no difference between samsara and nirvana.”

When ignorance ends, the same world appears as freedom.

3. “If you grasp the idea of emptiness, you destroy yourself.”

Even emptiness should not be clung to as a belief — it is experiential.

4. “The nature of things is to arise dependent on causes.”

Nothing exists independently; everything is interconnected.

5. “Those who see dependent origination see the Dharma.”

Wisdom comes from understanding the cause-effect web of life.
