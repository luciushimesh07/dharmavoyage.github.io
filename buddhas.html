Buddha

About Buddha

Gautama Buddha—born Siddhartha Gautama—became the Enlightened One after realizing the nature of suffering and the path to liberation. His teaching is based on mindfulness, compassion, and deep self-understanding. He rejected blind belief and taught that truth must be seen directly through one's own experience.

Five Quotes & Their Meanings

1. “Peace comes from within. Do not seek it without.”

External comforts never create lasting peace—inner wisdom does.

2. “What you think, you become.”

Your mind shapes your destiny and your character.

3. “The root of suffering is attachment.”

Clinging creates fear and dissatisfaction.

4. “Holding onto anger is like grasping a hot coal.”

Anger only hurts the one who holds it.

5. “Three things cannot be long hidden: the sun, the moon, and the truth.”

Truth reveals itself no matter how deeply it is buried.
