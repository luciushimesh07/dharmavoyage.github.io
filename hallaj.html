Mansur Al-Hallaj

About Mansur Al-Hallaj

Mansur Al-Hallaj (858–922) was a Sufi mystic known for his bold declaration, “Ana al-Haqq” (“I am the Truth”), expressing complete unity with the Divine. His life embodied devotion, fearlessness, and the radical dissolution of ego.

Five Famous Quotes

1) “I saw my Lord with the eye of the heart.”
Meaning: True vision comes from inner awakening, not physical sight.
2) “Between me and You, there is only me. Take away the me, so only You remain.”
Meaning: Ego is the only veil between the seeker and God.
3) “Your spirit is mingled with mine as wine with pure water.”
Meaning: Divine unity blends seamlessly with the human soul.
4) “I am He whom I love, and He whom I love is I.”
Meaning: The lover and the Beloved become one.
5) “Kill me, my faithful friends, for in my killing is my life.”
Meaning: Mystical death is the death of ego, leading to true life.
