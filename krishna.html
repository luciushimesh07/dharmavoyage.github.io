Krishna

Krishna is one of the most influential spiritual guides in world philosophy. His teachings in the Bhagavad Gita reveal the secrets of duty, devotion, selfless action, inner peace, and the true nature of the soul. He represents the perfect balance between wisdom and compassion, strength and softness, action and surrender.

Famous Quotes & Their Meaning

“You have the right to work, but never to the fruits of your actions.”

Krishna teaches detachment: focus on your effort, not the result. Freedom comes when your mind is not trapped by expectations.

“The soul is neither born, nor does it ever die.”

The true Self (Atman) is eternal and untouched by physical change. Knowing this removes fear, especially the fear of death.

“When meditation is mastered, the mind is unwavering like a flame in a windless place.”

A disciplined mind becomes steady and calm. This is the foundation of clarity, strength, and inner peace.

“A person is made by their belief. As they believe, so they become.”

Your thoughts shape your identity and destiny. Krishna emphasizes the power of mental purity and intention.

“Set your heart upon your duty, but never on the reward.”

True peace comes from doing what is right, not from craving reward. This is Karma Yoga — acting with sincerity and freedom.

Krishna’s Core Philosophy (Simplified)

Concept Explanation
Dharma (Duty) Act according to what is right, moral, and beneficial — not based on fear or desire.
Karma Yoga Perform actions selflessly. Action without attachment leads to liberation.
Bhakti (Devotion) Love and trust in the divine purify the mind and bring inner joy.
Atman (True Self) The soul is eternal, pure, and unchanging. Realizing this ends suffering.
Balance & Detachment Maintain equanimity in success and failure; this creates inner stability.
