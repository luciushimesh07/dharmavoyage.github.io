About Confucius

Confucius is one of China’s most influential philosophers, known for his teachings on virtue, ethics, responsibility, harmony, and moral leadership. His wisdom shaped society, governance, education, and personal conduct for centuries.

At the heart of Confucianism lies the belief that a good society comes from good individuals: people who cultivate integrity, kindness, discipline, and respect. His teachings are practical, grounded, and deeply humanistic.

Five Famous Quotes

“It does not matter how slowly you go as long as you do not stop.”

Steady, persistent progress matters more than speed.

“The man who moves a mountain begins by carrying away small stones.”

Great achievements begin with humble, consistent steps.

“Respect yourself and others will respect you.”

Self-worth becomes the foundation of harmonious relationships.

“To see what is right and not do it is the want of courage.”

Moral clarity must be matched by action.

“Wherever you go, go with all your heart.”

Full sincerity brings depth and beauty to life.

Confucius’ Philosophy — Summary Table

Concept Essence
Ren (Humaneness) Compassion, empathy, and kindness form the basis of moral living.
Li (Proper Conduct) Rituals, manners, and respectful behavior maintain harmony.
Yi (Righteousness) Doing what is right over what is convenient.
Junzi (The Noble Person) An ideal individual who embodies virtue, wisdom, and responsibility.
Education & Self-Cultivation Improvement through learning is a lifelong path to excellence.
